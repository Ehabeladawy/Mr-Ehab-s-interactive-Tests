Read the Passage Carefully:

The Arctic fox is a small animal that lives in very cold places. It has thick white fur in winter, which provides warmth and camouflage in the snow. When summer comes, its fur changes to brown or gray to match the rocks and plants. The Arctic fox eats small animals, birds, and sometimes fish. It can survive in freezing weather because of its warm fur and strong sense of smell. These adaptations help it find food and stay safe from larger animals.
